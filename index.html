Why It's Interesting

WASP-76b is so hot that iron can vaporize in its atmosphere. Scientists believe that on the cooler night side of the planet, the iron could condense and then rain down as liquid metal droplets.

Key Features

  • Day temperature of 2,400Â°C - hot enough to vaporize iron
  • Huge temperature difference between day and night
  • Tidally locked to its star (one side always faces sun)
  • Atmospheric winds transport vaporized metals
â­ Fun Fact

WASP-76b might have iron rain, making it one of the most extreme weather planets ever discovered. Imagine droplets of molten iron falling from the sky!

Why It's Interesting

Since 55 Cancri e orbits its star incredibly quickly (just 18 hours!), scientists believe that its surface is covered with oceans of molten lava. This super-Earth is larger than our planet but smaller than gas giants.

Key Features

  • Larger than Earth but smaller than gas giants
  • Completes full orbit in just 18 hours
  • Surface temperature 1,700-2,400Â°C on hot side
  • Possibly covered in lava oceans
â­ Fun Fact

TrES-2b is the darkest exoplanet ever discovered, reflecting less than 1% of the light that hits it. This makes it darker than coal or even black acrylic paint! The extreme darkness is still a mystery to scientists.

Key Features

  • Extremely low light reflection (<1%)
